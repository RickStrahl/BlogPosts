# The Free or Cheap Ride for Coding LLMs is Over
I guess the free dope phase in coding LLMs is over... Now comes the fun part of Christiane F., eh?

Looking for your next coding fix and getting more and more and more...


This week Anthropic announced big reductions in functionality for various of its paid plans - unannounced no less, limits just hit without warning - removing functionality like Claude Code from basic plans altogether and drastically reducing token budgets for more expensive subs. This after already pissing off developers who were complaining running out of tokens much more rapidly over the last few weeks especially with their newer models.

At the same time GitHub CoPilot announced that they would remove functionality in their existing plans, switch from request based budgets to the token budgets and bumping up model token multipliers specifically for the popular Claude Opus model which apparently is now getting completely removed.

## The Digital Prison
As much as a I love the technology and have enjoyed the benefits of these coding models, it's been clear for some time that the way these coding model plans were priced was never going to be sustainable. The way people were using these tools to do perform crazy complex operations and burning through tokens like M&Ms these plans were nothing but major loss leaders for the various providers. Heck, even with the pricing and functionality changes I wouldn't be surprised if that still is not making any money for anything but Enterprise licenses.

It's always been about trying to gain market share and capture users for future higher priced usage accounts. 

Don't think that this wasn't always the plan. Give you an irresistible taste, and then spring the big expensive paywall trap after you've seen what's possible... 

This tooling will be hobbled for the masses - just enough to keep on the juice, but not enough to do what most of us have been doing for the last year practically for pennies. Already you see daily accounts, of people proclaiming they never want to write another line of code and just use these models to do it for them.

It's a fucking addiction! There's no other way to describe it - once you catch the bug and you realize the possibilities it's hard to stop.

But now imagine getting priced out of use of that tooling? Or maybe you're not quite getting priced out but you're now competing against another developer who's multiple times your budget to leverage this technology. At that point it no longer is about any level of skill, but about how much money you can throw at this problem.

I think this last year will be remembered as one of those wonderful moments of freedom were people thought anything is possible and the sky's the limit... and then get shot down to the reality that there will be a (large) price attached to be able to do any of this - a cost that may actually not be that much cheaper than human labor after all, if priced to actual hardware and energy costs. 

And the good stuff - only available for those that can afford and big corps with limitless budgets. So much for AI being the great equalizer.

If not now, it's not long off because throwing away money was never the end game for the providers.

Maybe not today or tomorrow, but eventually the real cost of this technology will work its way into the pricing and it'll price most of us out leaving only big Enterprise and those that can afford it.

Be honest and think about what is a single run of Claude Opus to create an app, a module and major code cleanup. What is the value of that?

## Change?
If technology continues down this path of large online LLMs then that is a very likely outcome. There are those that say that technology will find a way - smaller, more efficient perhaps even running local models on the desktop. I'm not so sure. Technology - yeah that's a possibility, but there's a lot working against that ideology
